About Me

I am a student game developer. I started developing games in 2020 as my love of games took me to the next level. I have completed my NC and HNC Game Development courses and I am currently working towards my HND in game development at Edinburgh College.

This career step happened as I have been playing games for over 25 years and always wanted to create my own game. I have experience in different aspects of game development, although programming is where I have the most experience. Other areas that I have experience in are:

- Level Design.
- 2D Art and Design using programs like Aseprite and Photoshop. (Both bitmap images and Vector Images).
- 3D Modelling using Blender and 3DS Max.
- Video editing.
- Working within a team.
- Planning and Hosting events.

I have experience working in a few different programs. Most of my experiences lies within Visual Studio using Monogame. I am also very proficient using Unity. I also have experience working with Unreal Engine and Blueprint coding.

I love to create games that people can get lost in.

Games I Have Created

Bomber-Snakes

In this game I took inspiration from the classic games Snake and BomberMan

Each player controls a snake that can eat food to grow and pick up bombs
to blow up the breakable walls or the other snake.

The last player left in the game is the winner.

In this game I programmed:

- Step Movement
- Item Pickup
- Timed Spawning
- Counters
- Explosion Collisions
- Game States

SurgeBreaker

In this game I took inspiration from the classic game Breakout

The player bounces the ball to break bricks and keep the charge of the paddle up to continue play.

In this game I programmed:

- Horizontal Movement
- Bar Style Timer
- Different Style "Bricks"
- Game States

Pong Clone

I created this game in Unity to reinforce the basics of the C# programming language.

In this game I programmed:

- Vertical Movement
- Score Counter
- "AI" Style bat

Squid - Vasion

In this side-scrolling shooter, I took inspiration from the retro game Galaga.

Enemies spawn from the right hand side of the screen and move towards the player.

The player has to shoot the enemies to stop them getting to the left hand side of the screen.

In this game I programmed:

- Movement
- Shooting
- Collision
- Timed Spawning
- Life Counter
- Score Counter
- Game States

Dungeon Escape

In this game the player tries to escape a dungeon while avoiding the goblin capters.

Goblins wander around the dungeon making sure that everything is normal.

The player has to avoid being in line of sight of Goblins to successfully reach the end of the dungeon.

In this game I programmed:

- Tile Based Movement
- Line of Sight
- Collision
- Randomisation of movement.
- Life Counter
- Game States
